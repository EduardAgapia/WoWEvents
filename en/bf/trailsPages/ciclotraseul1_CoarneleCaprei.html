Coarnele Caprei Cycloroute

Description

The proposed cycling route is specific to the promotion of the Moldovan village that starts from the heart of the commune, Cohornele Caprei City Hall. After refueling at the grocery store (point hydration) and visiting the Orthodox Church, the route descends among traditional houses (some with motifs of naïve painting on the exterior). At the exit of the village (km 2), the cycling route Climb up to the viewpoint where you can admire the village. Then the route It winds through cereal fields (corn, wheat, sunflower, etc.), leaving behind on the right side a traditional sheepfold, after which it descends to Jijia Ponds and Miletin, where numerous birds (cormorants, wild ducks) can be observed, storks, egrets, etc.), as well as vegetation specific to them.The route follows the shore ponds, then intersect DJs, then climb gently up a valley to the sheepfold tourist Inn of Wolves. After a stop at the sheepfold for hydration, the cycling trail climbs on the road behind the sheepfold, crossing cereal fields, to the Fantanele forest (maximum altitude point – 158m), after which they descend to the village of Arama where they can be admired traditional houses with folk motifs. The last slope takes us to the center of of the commune, Cohornele Caprei City Hall, the end point of the cycloroute.

Trail length: 18 km
Vertical climb: 260 m
Maximum altitude: 158 m
Terrain: hilly (with slopes of 10-12%)
Road: DJ, DC (partly asphalt, gravel), farm roads
Degree of difficulty: Easy - Medium;
Recommended bike type: Mountain bike / Trekking;
Cyclist profile: Amateur/ Families with children/ Children +10 years
Journey time: 3 hours;
