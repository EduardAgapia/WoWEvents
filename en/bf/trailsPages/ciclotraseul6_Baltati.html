Bălțați Cycloroute

Description

The cycling route aims to highlight the natural and anthropogenic heritage of three villages components within Bălţaţi commune: Bălţaţi, Valea Oilor and Podișu. Departure is done from the goat farm Pui de Drag, located in the immediate vicinity of the sports base Balti. We follow the route to the two Orthodox churches in Balti, then leaving the village on the northern side we head towards the village Valea Oilor. From the entrance to the village We realize that in this village sheepfolds and flocks of sheep still make their presence felt at All the step, the locality being with an old tradition in this regard. We follow the descent on paved road to the accumulation in Valea Oilor, where we can observe flocks of ducks and geese, but also numerous wild birds (swans, wild ducks, egrets). Also here there is also the seat of a fish farm.Then we continue on a route of soil and grass up to the beautiful accumulation of Podișu, where we can observe herds of cows and flocks of sheep, after which on the edge of the lake we reach Podișu village with many elements of well-preserved traditional architecture. From here, we continue our route to the traditional sheepfold near Podișu. After a small tasting with quality dairy products (curd, urda, telemea), we continue the hilly route to the village of Războieni and then we do left on a farm road to Valea Oilor, to the village church, then we return to Balti village to the town hall, then to the final point of the cycloroute, Pui de Drag.

Vertical climb: 300 m
Maximum altitude: 162 m
Terrain: hilly (with slopes up to 8%)
Road: DC (partly asphalt, gravel), farm roads
Degree of difficulty: Medium;
Recommended bike type: Mountain bike / Trekking;
Cyclist profile: Trained amateur/ Families with children/ Children +10 years
Journey time: 2 1/2 - 3 hours;
