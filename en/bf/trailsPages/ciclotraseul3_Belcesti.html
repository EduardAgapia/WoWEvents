Belcești Cycloroute

Description

The cycling route aims to highlight to cyclists the natural and anthropogenic heritage of four villages in Belceşti commune: Belcești, Satu Nou, Munteni and Ulmi. The microzone is strategically located between several anthropogenic accumulations, which increases attractiveness of natural tourism potential. Departure is made in front of the city hall Belcești. For less trained cyclists, due to the longer length of cycloroute, it is recommended to go through the route in 2 stages or only one segment: Belcești – Poplar Lake – Munteni – Belcesti, respectively Belcești – Tansa Lake – Ulmi – Lițeni – Belcești. The first segment leads us from the center of the village to the old streets of the village to St. Theresa's Catholic Church, then stop at the Orthodox church and Satu Nou parish museum. From here, we follow the trail on the outskirts of the village where you can see a nice perspective on poplar accumulation. We walk on the parallel dirt road with the lake and then we cross the asphalt road that leads us to the village of Munteni, located on a hill. The streets of the village lead us to the village church of St. Archangels Michael and Gabriel, where you can admire an old wooden church. From here, the road we return to Belcești village, among old well-preserved traditional houses, some with naïve painting on the outside, but also numerous crosses. On the farm outside the village Belcești turns left on an agricultural exploitation road towards the Bahlui River. Here in the lower part of Belcești village we can admire flocks of ducks and geese, cross the river Bahlui on a small footbridge, after which we climb a small hill to cross paths with DJ Belcești – Spinoasa and continue our route to Lake Tansa, one of the largest lakes on the territory of Iasi County. On the edge of the lake we can admire the birds wild, until we reach the village of Ulmi which offers a beautiful view of the lake. It is worth making a stop at the museum point and the Memorial Ulmi – Poieni. From here, the road continues on the edge of the lake, past the village of Lițeni to the village of Belcești. We cross the old streets of the village among crosses and traditional houses, then we reach The center of the village near the town hall, the end point of our cycloroute.

Total length: 31 km
Vertical climb: 300 m
Maximum altitude: 162 m
Terrain: hilly (with slopes up to 8%)
Road: DJ, DC (partly asphalt, gravel), farm roads
Degree of difficulty: Medium;
Recommended bike type: Mountain bike / Trekking;
Cyclist profile: Trained amateur/ Families with children/ Children +12 years
Journey time: 3 1/2 - 4 hours;
