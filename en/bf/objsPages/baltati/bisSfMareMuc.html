Church of St. Demetrios the Great Martyr and Myrrhstreamer

Description

The church, dedicated to St. Demetrios, the Great Martyr and Myrrhstreamer, it was built between 1949 and 1956. On its place, in the village of Făcuţi (now Podișu) there was a cemetery chapel, built around 1919, which bore the same patron. A the ceasta was built of wood and was modified several times, but during the Second World War it was consumed by a strong and destroyed fire. The new church was consecrated on July 15, 1956, by His Eminence Theophilus of Roman and Husi, as attested by the "Commemorative Act" found in the church. Over time, it was rehabilitated and embellished with new icons and iconostasis, new bell, etc. In the churchyard was built a feast house and a parish house nearby, which was consecrated by His Eminence Theofan Metropolitan of Moldavia and Bucovina in 2012.

Location

Bălțați, Podișu village
