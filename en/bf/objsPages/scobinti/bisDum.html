All Saints' Sunday Church

Description

The construction of the church dedicated to All Saints' Sunday in the village of Scobinți was begun in 1850 by the former owner of the estate, Mihalache Cantacuzino Paşcanu. Started from Great foundations like a cathedral, it was raised only up to the vaults. Stone pillars have been calculated to support high towers, according to the will of the founder, who, however, has not completed construction until his death. The church has trifled shape with large, bright apses. The iconostasis, made in the 1980s, is carved in oak wood, and the interior is painted in 2010. But the icons are old, from 1850. On the occasion of the re-consecration, the church also receives the patronage of "The Holy Voivodes Michael and Gabriel". In the cemetery – in the middle, on a field elevation there is a cross that marks the place where the Dealul Voda Hermitage was, built of wood before 1740, by the monks of the Neamț monastery.

Location

Scobinți, Scobinți village
