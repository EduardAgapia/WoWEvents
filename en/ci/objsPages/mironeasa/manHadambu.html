Hadâmbu Monastery

Description

Hadâmbu Monastery is an Orthodox monastery in Romania, founded by the Greek boyar Iani Hadâmbul in 1659 in the village schitu Hadâmbului, Mironeasa commune (Iaşi county). The Ensemble of Hadâmbu Monastery was included on the List of historical monuments in the county Iasi since 2015, with LMI code IS-II-a-A-04242 and consists of the following 3 Objectives:
- Church "Nativity of the Mother of God" - dating from the period 1659, having the code IS-II-m-A-04242.01;
- The entrance tower - dating from the XVII century, having the code IS-II-m-A-04242.02;
- Enclosure wall - dating from the XVII century, having the code IS-II-m-A-04242.03.
Hadâmbu Monastery is a fortified complex, representative for architecture Moldovan from the XVII century. The enclosure wall has a rectangular plane, being provided at the corners with circular towers, specific to constructions with functions of defense (fortresses) of that time. The tower at the entrance had both the role of an entrance tower, as well as the bell tower.

Location

Mironeasa, Schitu Hadâmbului village
