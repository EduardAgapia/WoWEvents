Cetatea Monastery

Description

100 years ago, in the village of Dagâța, the towers of the Cetătuia Monastery were erected (another than the one in Iasi), founded by the Holy Voivode Stephen the Great, as he says local tradition.
Protected on each side naturally, Cetățuia – the name of the wooded hill located in the west of the Gârbeşti village, with an altitude of 467 m, being also known under the the name of the Great Fortress.
According to the researchers, in 1843 the Cetatea hermitage was active, having a number of 7 monks.
In 1865, the disappearance of the hermitage was recorded, as a result of the Law secularization of monastic properties, given by The Ruler AL. I. Cuza. The Hermitage has was rebuilt in 1969 by the citizens of the villages Tansa, Mănăstirea, Gârbeşti, Suhuleţ, Runcu, Piscu, Veja, Stanita, Tamija.
Like other monasteries, the Fortress Monastery also suffered persecution of the communist period, but with God's help, after 1989 it was reactivated with the blessing of His Grace Daniel, Metropolitan of Moldavia and Bucovina, becoming thus an oasis of Orthodox peace and spirituality.

Location

Dagâța, located on the Fortress hill, 5 km from Mânăstirea village
