Zimbru Hermitage

Description

The church of the Zimbru hermitage in Pânceşti commune, Neamţ County was built between the years 1954-1965 by a local of the area named Constantin Voinescu with the contribution of believers in the surrounding villages. The church was built by wooden forks of oak, leaches and leaches and clay, and the roof made of shingles. The works were completed on 29.06.1965. The church was consecrated on 14.09.1965 by His Grace Bishop Partenie Ciopron, dedicated to "ST. Emperors Constantine and Helen". In the beginning of the it functioned as a chrism church until 1994 being a filie of Pâncești parish. Since 01.04.1994 until now it functions as a hermitage of monks with the name Zimbu Hermitage. On 21.05.2008, near the current hermitage Zimbru His Grace Ioachim Bacaunul, Vicar Archbishop of the Diocese of Roman laid the foundation stone of a new churches dedicated to the Protection of the Mother of God. The old documents of the chancelleries medieval remembered the presence in the area of a hermitage of monks, specified by the most many times as a landmark or as a decision of the estates owned by the Diocese of Roman.

Location

Pâncești, Pâncești village
