Bârnova Monastery

Description

Bârnova Monastery is located 10 km Southeast of Iași. This was built between 1626 and 1666 by its two founders, the voivodes Moldovans Miron Barnovschi and Eustratie Dabija, on the site of an old wooden church, which dated from 1603 and which was dedicated to the Holy Great Martyr George. From the year 1663, when the Monastery was dedicated to the Patriarchate of Jerusalem, the holy abode was administered by Greek monks for 200 years.
On September 15, 1863, the estates and assets of the monastery become the property of the state, while the palace of Mrs. Dana is transformed into a village school and a house Parish. Between 1908 and 1945 it has the status of a parish, and from 1945 to 1950 it is monastery of nuns. From 1950 to 1990, the monastery has again the status of a parish, and in July 1991 the Metropolitanate of Moldavia and Bucovina restored its status as a monastery of Monks.
Currently, the monastery counts 13 inhabitants, who are engaged in painting icons, making candle rafts and incense. The holy abode preserves the traditional ordinance of church services, with the seven Daily Praises.

Location

Bârnova, Bârnova village
