Paleontological Reserve - Repedea Hill

Description

The Repedea Paleontological Reservation is located on the Repedea hill, where there is a outcrop and quarries, which bring to light calcareous rocks and sandstones fossiliferous limestones, which belong to the Sarmatian (about 10 million years). The existing fossils and limestones attest that on this territory was the seabed Sarmatic. Of the fossils, the most important are those of Mactra Podolica. The Repedea Hill is also important in terms of vegetation, fauna, morphology the relief and the landscape, due to its position, at the border of the Central Plateau Moldovan with the Moldavian Plain and the contact between the forest-steppe and the forest.
The Repedea Geological and Paleontological Reserve currently stretches over a surface of 47.47 hectares. Traces of the withdrawal of the Sarmatic Sea - the skeletons of cemented shells that have become an integral part of the hill and the animal fossils are more visible in areas of Pietrarie, where the walls are formed from layers of sediment.

Location

Bârnova, Pietrăria village
