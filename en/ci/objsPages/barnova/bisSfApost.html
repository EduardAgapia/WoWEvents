"St. Apostles Peter and Paul" Church

Description

The church was built in 1812 by the founders Spiridon Papadopol and his wife Ruxandra, with their daughter Anastasia and son-in-law Anastasie Căminas. The bones of the founders are buried in the nave, and the portraits are preserved in the narthex.
The church is built of stone covered with black atoll in the form of a plan basilical, with arched altar. Above the narthex there is a turret that also serves to the bell tower in which three bells are housed. The interior of the church is covered by a semi-cylindrical vault. The painting on the vault of the altar and the nave were executed by anonymous painters in 1944. The painting of the icons in the iconostasis is from the age of NINETEENth. The iconostasis is made of fir wood with floral ornaments, and the doors royalty from elements of sculpture.
In the churchyard there was a parish house that served as a primary school in the year 1917 to 1923. Restoration works were carried out in 1888 by the efforts of the priest Ioan Dimitriu Bobulescu, when some changes were made, such as: the exterior walls were raised much higher, the windows were enlarged, and the canopy-shaped porch was built at the same time, increasing its capacity inner of the church.

Location

Bârnova, Păun village
