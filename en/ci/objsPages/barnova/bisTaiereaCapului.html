The Church "Cutting off the Head of St. John the Baptist"

Description

The church "Cutting off the Head of St. John the Baptist" was founded by the sons of man political Ion C. Inculet (1884 - 1940). Built of brick on the family domain Inculet with an area of about 2.5 hectares, the church is imposing by architecture and exude spirituality and exhortation to faith, dominating the surroundings because it is located on a hill.
The church is painted in wax, with a special and unique theme, the painting being made in 1947 by Professor Ştefan Constantinescu from the Faculty of Belle Arte from Bucharest. The church was sanctified in 1947, and the following year, as a result of the will of the founders, the chapel together with the land owned by the Inculet family was donated to the Metropolitanate of Moldavia and Bucovina by the descendants of the boyar Ion Inculet.

Location

Bârnova, Bârnova village
