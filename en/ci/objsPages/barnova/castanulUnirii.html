The chestnut of the union

Description

The chestnut tree in Bârnova is now almost 250 years old, well above the average life of such a of the tree.
The local community celebrates it every year on May 25, the day it took place the first meeting of the unionists. On May 25, 1856, a group of 21 unionists, led by Mihail Kogălniceanu and Vasile Alecsandri laid the foundations of a historical act - Unirea Romanian Principalities. Far from those hostile to the union, they met under a chestnut tree, in the village of Vişan, in the present commune of Bârnova. "They met outside the city, for that, in the city, the ears of the spies of Mihai Sturza and others, who were not favorable Unirii, would have quickly scattered such meetings. I would have taken the chase on those who had dangerous ideas inconsistent with the wishes of the reign or Russia neighboring," explains historian Sorin Iftimi.
The chestnut became a symbol of the Union of the Principalities in 1859. On May 25, 1856, Vasile Alecsandri even wrote a poem, the lyrics becoming an oath of allegiance for this desideratum of the Romanians:
"Under these great chestnut
We all swear with brotherhood,
That today there will be no more
Neither Wallachian nor Moldovan;
But let's be only Romanians
In a thought, in a union,
And let's shake hands with our hands
For the country's happiness."
