Faur's Mound

Description

The story "The Mound of Faur" sheds light on the age of the nation on these places. Sharing the opinion of several historians, the writer C. Nonea notes that "these mounds were not lifted from the earthquake, at random , but were lifted with sweat, sighing and crushing of bones, of the natives of these lands, who defended their hearth, and the insatiable wealth of the predatory invaders" and are of a kind with the Moldavian nation. The writer painted the people from the place, on the "peasants from father to son" who have learned not to sleep " more than five hours a night because they are twinned with work from their fields", and the nights seem so short that " they do not have time to feel the piscatul flea, so it's the noises."
