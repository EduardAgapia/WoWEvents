The Camp of Şipote - Considered by the survivors of the camp as the Hell of Şipote

Description

At the end of 1916, the Central prisoners arrived in large numbers at Shipote. Whereas the barracks were insufficient to accommodate them all, some of them were deposited in open shelters. They were treated very badly, hit by gendarmes and guards, sometimes on the orders of officers. A large transport of prisoners, started from Tecuci and consisting of 8,000 men, arrived at On December 24, 1916. The road had been made on foot for 10 days, under the conditions an early winter, of sloppy roads, the prisoners being hit, including with the whip, and fed stupid. The number of prisoners would explode a few months later, in January 1917. reaching 17,000. Captain doctor Pasztor, an Austrian prisoner, was seconded from Dobrovat at Şipote in 1917, during the epidemics, and would remain there until spring 1918.
The number of central prisoners dead at Şipote, "certified" by the Romanian authorities in In 1918, it would have risen to 6,577. With the 5,453 "missing", a total of 12,030 would result in a total of 12,030 of prisoners who found their end in the Camp of Shipote in the years 1916-1918, that is, two thirds of those interned there.

(Text taken from the article of the historian Dorin Dobrincu published on Free Europe in a series entitled "Central Prisoners of War and Civilian Interneates in Romania, 1916-1918")
