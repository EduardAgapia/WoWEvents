The ruins of the court of the boyar Luca Arbore from Şipote

Description

History: The goalkeeper of Suceava from the time of the great voivode Stephen the Great and Holy and counselor for the next two rulers of Moldavia (Bogdan cel Orb and Ştefăniță Vodă) was the owner of the village Şipote in the second part of the XV century and the beginning of the XVI century.
The boyar's court of Luca Arbore was built in 1507, with the church of St. John the New (both buildings are included in the List of Historical Monuments and are part of the Court's Ensemble Boyar of Luca Arbore (ruins).
Luca Arbore was accused of treason by the voivode in 1523 and beheaded at Hârlău. All his properties were confiscated. The boyar court of Şipote was transformed into princely detour, then in the royal residence.
