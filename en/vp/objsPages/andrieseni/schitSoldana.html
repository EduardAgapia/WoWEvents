Șoldana Hermitage

Description

The Church of the Soldan Monastery, originally a hermitage, was built in 1828, at the boyar's court of the family Floroaia. The place of worship is built in the form of a ship of brick wall, on the foundation of raw stone career. The painting was made in 1955 by the painters Gheorghe Simionica, from Flămânzi and D. Aniculesei from Botoşani. After the Second World War the abode of worship was affected and went into restoration, being consecrated in 1948. In 1961, in the period to the communist regime, the hermitage is closed, and the nuns were chased away. After 1990, the hermitage was reopened in the form of a monastery. The church was in rehabilitation between 2005 and 2014, when consolidation works were carried out, it was carried out changed the porch and was built a iconostasis made of oak wood. Currently, within the The monastery's are five monks, one of whom is the father of the starosta.
