Șoldana Train station, LMI code: IS-II-m-B-04097

Description

Șoldana Railway Station is one of the monumental buildings built at the end of the XIX century along Railways of Moldova. The Iași - Dorohoi line, on which this railway station is located, was built between 1891 and 1896 and commissioned on 1 August 1896. Like other impressive train stations on this route, the train station The hip was built by the "Concession of the engineer Ioan G. Cantacuzino" after the studies and projects developed under the direction of engineer Elie Radu. The massiveness of the unique volume and the unobtrusive central part highlighted in rezalit reminds of the proportions of the Mogosoaia palace. The roof in 2 waters brings slender assembly, especially evident at the lateral gables. Brick of Ciurea, used for the construction of the most beautiful railway stations in the old Kingdom (Moldavia and Wallachia) and left apparent, helps to put in their value by the nobility and vigor of the material, namely by the exceptional quality of clay by Ciurea. Thus, this station fits perfectly into the national style of railway architecture through expressiveness and minerality.
