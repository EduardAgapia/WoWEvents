Church of the Nativity of St. John the Baptist

Description

The church dedicated to "The Birth of St. John The Baptist" was built in 1924, with the help of believers in the villages of Vulturi and Hunters and two merchants from Iaşi (Iancu Săndulache and Vasile Antonesei), but he became parish only in 1942. The church was erected on a stone foundation and has the shape of a ship, with two lateral apses. Above the porch is located the bell tower, 11 m high. Inside, the iconostasis is made of linden wood and does not have sculptures or ornaments. Because of the earthquake since 1940, the church has deteriorated, so it has been rebuilt after the war in 1945. Over time, several maintenance works were carried out and repair of the church, and in 1979 the parish house was built and in 1986 a house of celebration. In 1994, the church was honored with the presence of Her Excellencies Queen Anne and Elisabeth, during visits at the former Roznovanu Mansion in cotu morii commune (Victoria).

Location

Popricani, Vulturi village
