Stânca-Roznovanu former palace

Description

In the forest of Stanca village there was, a few decades ago, an imposing castle built by treasurer Iordache Rosetti-Roznovanu. It was erected in the early nineteenth century on the site a boyar manor dated from 1630. Roznovanu Palace prevailed between 1850 - 1940, here balls and parties of the great boyar were organized: in 1803 a ball was organized in his honor Alexandru Moruzzi, on the occasion of the Tsar's day, and in 1833 and 1834 here was celebrated the arrival of the ambassador Turkish Musir Ahmed Pasha in Iaşi. Other distinguished guests were Kushnicov (he spent the whole summer at castle) and Krasno-Milashevich, who successively succeeded in the leadership of the Divan between 1808 - 181217. Historian Radu Rosetti describes Iordache as "a very rich man, who liked to keep a house great and carry on a special luxury." In the Palace Park was built a church, and about the bell it is said to have had silver in its composition.

Location

Victoria, Stânca village
