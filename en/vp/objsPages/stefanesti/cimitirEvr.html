Jewish cemetery of Ștefănești

Description

The Jewish cemetery is located at the exit of the village Ştefănești. In Hebrew it is called Beit Haim, in free translation meaning house Life. Among the many tombs belonging to the Jews of Ştefănești is found and the tomb of the Holy Rabbi, The Goat A.M. Friedman (1848-1933). In 1834, the goat Nahum Friedman created "The Rabbinic Court of Ștefănești", which will become the center of Hasidism in Moldova and will orient the Jewish community towards Ștefănești, thus that, for example, in 1859 in Ştefănești were 1457 Jews, and in 1886 their number increases to 3886. An important presence was also Rabbi Bezalel Zaev Safran, who led the community until 1905, making important contributions to the field of Jewish studies.

Location

Ștefănești, Ștefănești village
