Church of St. Pious Parascheva

Description

The church was dedicated to "St. Cuv. Parascheva" of was built in the seventeenth century (1640), by to Stephen II Tomșa. The place of worship is partially built on the foundation of the church "St. Procopia", which is said to have been erected by Stephen the Great. It was made at much larger sizes compared to the Stefanian style, having a defensive character. The entrance is in the Gothic style. Under the church is found a tunnel believed to have been used as a hideout. Several works have been done repair in the years 1793, 1794 (rebuilding the tower bell tower), 1811, 1828. It is included in the List of Historical Monuments with the code BT-II-a-B-02019.

Location

Ștefănești, Ștefănești village
