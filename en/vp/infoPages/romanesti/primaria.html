The Romanian Commune Hall and the Cultural Center „Maria Lătărețu”

Description

Being also called "The Nightingale of Gorj", Maria Lătărețu was a singer of peasant music and folk from Gorj. He has held numerous concerts with famous conductors and orchestra heads such as Radu Voinescu, Constantin Mirea, Ionel Budişteanu and many others. The death of the singer is related to the village Romanian. In 1972, being on a tour with Ion Lucian, Laura Lavric and Ionel Schipoancă, the artist dies from a brain congestion, while giving the recital at the cultural home Româneşti, Jud. Botoşani. Maria Lătărețu is buried at the "Sfânta Vineri" cemetery in Bucharest. In memory of this event, at the entrance to the cultural home in Romania was placed a plaque memorial, and the Cultural House bears its name today
