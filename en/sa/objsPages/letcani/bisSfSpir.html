„Saint Spiridon” Church

Description

The church "Saint Spiridon" has an unusual shape due to its round prefect plan, which is what what makes it a unique church in Romania and one of the few circular churches in the world. The shape of the church is attributed to the influence of Russian neoclassicism, which appeared at the end of the eighteenth century.By its round plan, this place of worship is similar to the church built by Empress Helena of Byzantium above the tomb of Jesus Christ from Jerusalem. This architectural style spread from Jerusalem to the West, reaching also in Romania.The church does not have large dimensions. The entrance is through the west side, through a portico consisting of four columns with ionic capes, above which rises a small steeple. A number of consoles support the cornice, this style being found at other houses in Iasi, such as the Notre Dame de Sion Institute, which dates back to about the same era. In the part of east, the altar has a semicircle shape and a height lower than that of the circular plane, being attached to the great circle of the church. In the apse of the altar there is another entrance to the church. The iconostasis of the church dates from the end of the XIX century – the beginning of the XXth century- th, the icons being worked by the painter Gh. Ionescu from Iaşi (1908-1909) in Byzantine style.
