The marble monument of the parents of the poet Dimitrie Anghel

Description

Corneşti village is indisputably linked to the name of the Anghel family although it is historically attested from the year 1613. Boyar Anghel tried in the nineteenth century to bring Italians to work Earth. He introduced modern agricultural technology for those times. Dimitrie Anghel, son the boyar is well known in Romanian literature as the Poet of Flowers. The village school is it bears the name and a bust of the poet is placed in front of the building. The mansion of the Anghel family it was transformed during the communist period in the C.A.P. headquarters, remaining a museum room. After 1989 the mansion was demolished by villagers who saw in the building only the symbol of collectivization forced agriculture. Miroslava City Hall plans to restore it. A velvet box that is find out on the work desk of the poet was recovered and donated to the National Museum of Literature Novels from Iasi by Ștefan Susai, writer and researcher, author of monographs about Miroslava. In the courtyard of the cornesti church there is the marble monument of the parents of the poet Dimitrie Anghel. The white marble monument is of interest to visitors as well as the Buna church Proclamation, built in 1833 by Archbishop Filaret Beldiman. The poet is buried at the Eternitatea cemetery in Iași.

Location

Miroslava, Cornești village
