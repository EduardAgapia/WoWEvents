Horlești Church

Description

On November 19, 1871, in response to the manifestation of the desire of the believers in Horlesti to build a church, diocesan bishop Joseph Camilli shows that this is necessary, the church old, wooden is in danger of tearing itself down over believers during office religious. On April 6, 1872, Simhe Isacu from the hamlet of Horlesti declares before the local authorities that he is hired by Fr. Vincenzo Valenza to make 80,000 bricks by July 1. The construction of the church was completed in 1873, and as patron was elected St. Joseph. After 1913 in the stone foundation of the church appeared more and more pronounced cracks. They have massive infiltration of water has been revealed. The repairs were multiple, complicated and expensive: the result was minimal and short-lived. In 1965 the edifice of the church was repaired. Church i- two side wings were attached and in front a brick tower with reinforced concrete accounts, on the stone foundation. A significant part of the necessary stone was brought from the foundation of the old churches before 1871. In 1967 the renovation works were completed. The tower has a height of 22 m, and the body of the church has a length of 23 m and a width of 18 m.
