Jewish cemetery in Răducăneni

Descriere

Cthe Jewish cemetery from Răducăneni village is located about 2.2 km from the center of the village, in an area of hill, representing a proof of the existence of the people Jew at Răducăneni in the NINETEENth century. Cemetery occupies an area of 2.62 hectares. Documents attests that the Jews came to Răducăneni on to 1830, at the invitation of hetman Răducanu Roset, to build here a shopping center, located at half the road between Iaşi and Huși. In the cemetery it is find 570 Jewish tombs, most of them tombstones having engraved on the face of the number the plot to which they belong. The oldest stone of Tomb dates back to 1867, and the most recent (and at the same time the last) from 1983. In the past, at the entrance in the cemetery there was a chapel ("ohel" in Hebrew), used to prepare the bodies for burial ("tahara"), but also for memorial services Short. Next to it was an unmarked tombstone believed to have been the place where the prayer books or other sacred manuscripts were buried. In 2011 there was a process of restoration, cleaning and cataloguing of tombstones in the cemetery.
