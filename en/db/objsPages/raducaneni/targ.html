Răducăneni – multicultural fair

Description

Răducăneni was founded in 1838 during the reign of Prince Mihail Sturdza on Răducanu's estate Roset (from which he also took the name) to promote trade in the countryside. For this a encouraged the establishment here of foreign merchants, most of them Jews coming from Galicia and Poland. In 1844, Catholics (also called the Csangos) were brought from the land of Roman by the boyar Constantine Rosetti, who settled at the NE of the Bohotin valley, forming a Catholic nucleus around the church that dates from the XIX century, but also gypsy slaves, who settle in the same part of the hearth. In 1907, during the Uprising, the local population was dissatisfied with the price imposed by the Jewish tenant Froim Fişer for an earth jaw (1.5 ha) – 130 lei. Rumours of agitations and carnage in villages in the country they generated fear among the Jewish population, on the one hand, but also an anxiety and pips of revolt among the peasants. In order to prevent the degeneration of events, the Catholic Bishop of Iasi, Camilli, sends the parish priest of Husi to urge the Catholic community in Răducăneni to calm down. His speech managed to convince the local population that the best way is dialogue and he managed to convinces the leaseholder to halve the price. Being pleased, the local Catholics decided a few days later, upon hearing some rumors that a gang of 600 gypsy robbers would come wants to come to set fire to the city, to stand to defend the fair with rifles, stumps and revolvers.
