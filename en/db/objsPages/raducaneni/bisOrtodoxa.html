"All Saints' Sunday" Orthodox Church

Description

The church dedicated to "All Saints' Sunday" was built between 1900-1905. The founder is Dumitru Goian, the son of the priest Constantin Goian, about whom tells that he studied in Iasi, where, being noticed for his voice, he became a psalt, later a protopsalt. Due to the modest life he led saved enough to erect a church and school in his native village. The church is built on the site of an old one, about which it is knows that it was made of wood and was brought from Bessarabia to Bohotin and later moved to Roşu, Bâzga, and finally to Răducăneni. The current place of worship is built of carved stone in style Moldovan and is painted inside and in the open porch

Location

Răducăneni, Răducăneni village
