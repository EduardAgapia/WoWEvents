Catholic Church Saints Apostles Peter and Paul

Description

The church dedicated to "St. Apostles Peter and Paul" was raised from 1914 to 1938 to serve the Catholic population that has it was displaced from around roman. The construction was carried out in a combination of the Roman and Gothic styles according to the plans architect Jenric Jasz from Bucharest. Of execution was handled by the entrepreneur Lorenzo Collavigni from Huşi with stonemason craftsmen brought from Italy. The place of worship is distinguished by the fact that it is painted on the interior in style Renaissance with Byzantine shades. In the church sculptures of both St. Arch are present. Michael and Gabriel, as well as the apostles Peter, Paul, Andrew and John, and the central altar is made of white marble. The bells, the organ and the clock were brought from different parts of Germany. After earthquakes of 1977 and 1986, consolidation works were carried out.

Location

Răducăneni, Răducăneni village
