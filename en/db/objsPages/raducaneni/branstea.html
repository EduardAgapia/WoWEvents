The princely forest from Bohotin and the old Bohotin Monastery (extinct)

Description

The gentlemen's own domains, used mainly for hunting, represented the brawls princely. They belonged exclusively to the lord and could be used by him at his will. At the same time, the princely fir tree means "a place with a rare forest, covered with grassy vegetation". The area that was called Braniştea from Bohotin was one of the most important from the point of view of view of the practice of beekeeping in Moldova of the XVth century. Incidentally, beekeeping deals with approximately the third place in the revenues charged on the tax authorities on the products. Braniştea stretched from Ungheni to Grozeşti. In this area there was already, before it became a princely bravado, Bohotin Monastery, attested in 1411, during the reign of Alexander the Good. Then it is mentioned that "the monastery of Bohotin will listen to this monastery" (Bistrita). Some historians have considered it as one of the oldest church buildings in Moldova. The last mention of it is from the time of Stephen the Great, in 1462. The place where this monastery would have been located has not yet been Discovered.

Location

Răducăneni, Bohotin village
