”Saint Varvara” Church

Description

The church from Şorogari village dedicated to The Holy Martyr Varvara is at the outskirts of Şrogari village. In the beginning it was a church made of wooden beams founded by the Greek monks from Golia Monastery in Iaşi in 1725, at the perseverance of Constantine, old protopsalt of the MetropolitanAte of Moldova and Sucevei. The church was repaired in 1830 by Ştefan Guriţă, formerly leaseholder of the Golia estate. Arrived in a state of ruin, was built again, under ship shape, of brick and stone on the foundation of the old church and was covered with tin with the help of the village parishioners Şrogari, headed by the epitrope Leon G. Coroi and the donations granted by the Metropolitan church of Moldova and Suceava, being consecrated in 1925. The church was renovated in 1937, and in 1943, when it was rehabilitated for the 3rd time they decided to be re-consecrated.

Location

Aroneanu, Șorogari village
