Church of the Holy Archangels Michael and Gabriel

Description

The old church in Mircești was originally built by a group of monks, in 1532. It has been translocated twice. The original location was on a place called Grui. Because the village was threatened by the invasion of the Tatars, the village was moved, together with the church, in the forest nearby to a place called Silişte. The second time, the village was found by the Tatars so the church along with the village was moved to the center of the same forest. Initially, the church was covered with reeds, later it was covered with shingles, and then with tin. Until 1907, it did not have an interior painting, being made in that year by the painter Nicolae Dimitriu, together with a iconostasis. Larger repairs have been made to 1864 and 1906. With the erection of the new church, in the old one only the funerals and the Mass are made on the occasion of the feast day of St. Arch. Michael and Gabriel.

Location

Tăcuta, Mircești village
