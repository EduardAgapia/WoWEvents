The parental home of Ion Mitican

Description

Ion Mitican (1931-2012) was an engineer and Romanian writer, born in Dăneşti commune, Romania Vaslui County, in a family of modest peasants. He graduated from primary school in the locality of his native, then attended high school in Iasi, at The National High School, which he had to interrupt because of the war (1944) and which he continued them in Vaslui. In 1954, it graduated from the Faculty of Electrical Engineering of the Polytechnic Institute of Iasi. Began to he worked as an electrical engineer in Vaslui, then he worked as an electrical engineer in Vaslui, then he moved to Regionala CFR Iaşi. Ion Mitican has been passionate about history since his student years, researching works of historians who wrote about the city of Iasi, which led him in time to write about Iasi some other time. He wrote 22 books about places, happenings, old buildings in Iasi and published hundreds of articles in local magazines and newspapers, such as "Flacăra Iașului" (80s) or Lumina Newspaper" (2007 - 2010). Ion Mitican died in March 2012 in Iasi and was buried in the "Petru şi Pavel" cemetery in the city. His parental home is located in Dăneşti commune, between Emil Racoviţă and Bereasa villages.

Location

Dănești, between Emil Racoviță and Bereasa villages
