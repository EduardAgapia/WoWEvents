The parental home of the doctor Grigore T. Popa

Description

The parental home of acad. Grigore T.Popa is located 500 m from the church, on the way to cemetery, on the left side. Doctor Grigore T. Popa he was born in 1892 in Șurăneşti (today the village of Emil Racovita, Dăneşti commune). Specialized Anatomist Popa worked on popularizing science modern, to the reform of education systems medical and superior and, in war hospitals, as practitioner decorated and appreciated publicly. Helped the training of a generation of top doctors as holder of the Department of Anatomy and Embryology at Institute of Anatomy Iasi of the Faculty of Medicine in Iasi (1928). The value of his discoveries in the field of neuroendocrine relations, the pituitary port system, provided him with worldwide recognition. Professor Popa is the one who advised George Emil Palade to continue his studies in England or America, a decision that earned Palade the Nobel Prize. In 1936, together with Mihail Sadoveanu and George Topârceanu, founded the magazine Însemnări Iaşine, a prestigious publication in the Romanian culture. In memory of the great doctor, the University of Medicine and Pharmacy in Iasi bears his name, and since the year 2011, dănești school also decides to be named in memory of the academician.

Location

Dănești, Emil Racoviță village
