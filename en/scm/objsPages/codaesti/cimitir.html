Jewish cemetery

Description

The Codăeşti Fair was founded around 1838 by the Jews who were banished from the province of Pocuţia (today Ukraine). After 100 years, the number of Jews in the locality had reached over 400. In Codăeşti it is find two Jewish cemeteries, an old one (with about 100 tombstones from the period 1865- 1895) and a new one (with about 180 tombstones from 1895 to 1960). Following a earthquake occurred a landslide that covered or destroyed some of the graves. Not being cared for for a long time, some inscriptions become unintelligible. Currently in Codăeşti no longer there are Jewish families. The main problems of cemeteries are: lack of signaling, vegetation that cover the tombs, almost half are fallen or bent. Most of them are rectangular, made of limestone or sandstone, with inscriptions in Hebrew and/or Romanian.

Location

Codăești, Rediu-Galian village
