Jewish cemetery

Description

The cemetery in Lespezi was established in the nineteenth century, and the last known burial is in 1937. The cemetery is located near the Siret River, outside the village, and is surrounded by a stone wall. There are about 200 tombstones carved in marble, sandstone or limestone, most of them collapsed or broken, surrounded by dense vegetation. The inscriptions are in Hebrew and Romanian, and some Stone slabs have traces of painting, decorations made of iron or bronze.

Location

Lespezi, Lespezi village
