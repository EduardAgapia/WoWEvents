Lespezi Train station

Description

Lespezi railway station is part of the series of railway stations built on the Roman-Suceava (Iţcani) line by the Concession Offenheim (consortium of Austrian and English bankers represented by the Knight Victor von Offenheim) between the years 1869-1871. The train station in Lespezi is identical to the stations Leorda and Bucecea (on the line Vereşti – Botoşani) and has neo-gothic elements at the level of cornice and attic treatment. Based on symmetry and centrality, this model of the building, with a shorter length than the "sisters" from Vereşti, Botoşani or Roman, presents a unitary, linear façade on the side of the platform and a slightly rezalit, with a gable on the opposite side, towards the street.

Location

Lespezi, Heci village
