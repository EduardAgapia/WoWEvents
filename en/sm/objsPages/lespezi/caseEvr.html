Description

After the establishment of the fair, in the first half of the nineteenth century, each merchant was entitled to a lot 5 m wide and 40 m long for setting up the shop. Due to this openness to trade, The Jewish community of Lespezi had reached over 50% of the population of the fair after 1850. These were mostly merchants and craftsmen. Before World War II, in the Lespezi fair, apart from glass factory there were six bakeries and 17 restaurants, three of which had orchestras (La Costache Zidu, La Maier and the restaurant canteen has glass factory).
