Cotnari Fortress - Cătălina Hill

Description


RAN code: 96487.02
Location: At the NW edge of the village
Dating: Hallstatt (inc. IV century A. Chr.) - Fortress, La Tène late (II - III century) - Fortress
The Thracian-Getae Fortress of Catalina is one of the most important discoveries in this area of the country. A was an old hearth of rural and city habitation, fortification and strategic point of observation and defense, a true political and military center of thraco-Getic civilization. Located at an altitude of 395m, it dominates better of 200m difference in level the surrounding areas, providing excellent visibility in all directions. Between 1967-1985, the fortress was thoroughly researched by the archaeologist Adrian C. Florescu. In 1985 they started the works of preservation in situ of the Thraco-Getic Fortress Cătălina. It was covered with a fiber covering glass, with a semihyperboloid-shaped vault (60m x 20m), a unique work at that time. In the Cotnari area there would have been, in the Getic period, an important center of power consisting of the following fortresses: the one on the Cătălina Hill, the one from Buhalnița - "Cetate", the one from the hearth of the Horodiştea village, the one from the Horodiştea village, the ones from the area of Scobinţi - "Dealul lui Vodă" and "Dealul Basaraba", to which would be added the fortress from Todireşti - "Dealul Şanţurilor". From the subsequent analyses it was found that the fortification on the Cătălina Hill, named precinct A, makes a common body with the one in the hearth of horodiștea village (precinct B), resulting in a single fortress of over 70 ha. Inside enclosure A, the density of the remains is reduced.
