Despot Voda's Latin School in Cotnari

Description

The foundation stone of the college from Cotnari was laid in the summer or autumn of 1562. Building the college was built near the great Catholic church in Cotnari, but the foundations of this school did not still been discovered. Despot voda strove to gather teachers from all over Europe, but in special from Wittenberg, Germany. One of the professors of the college was Johannes Sommer Pirnensis, who also wrote about Despot's life. The college was destined for the reformed population of Moldova, from the rows of which the students were recruited, and here will be "the place of preparation of the future clergy Protestant." The Reformed were Saxons and Hungarians, living in the cities of Moldavia, who had chosen the new faith, just like the Germans and Hungarians in Transylvania. Among the teachers there were no children from the Orthodox environment, because the Orthodox looked with suspicion and enmity at the renewals that Despot had brought in the voda Moldova. The language of instruction was Latin, and Despot also took care of the organization of a library. College and the library were destroyed in the fall of 1563, when Despot voda was also killed. Nobility and clergy in Moldova viewed Protestant propaganda with hostility. College was seen as a danger to "religion" homeland"
