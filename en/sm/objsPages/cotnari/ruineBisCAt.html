Ruins of St. Mary's Catholic Church in Cotnari

Description


RAN code: 96487.05
LMI code: IS-I-s-A-03562
Dating: Medieval Era (XV century)
Of the four Catholic churches existing in 1599, the largest was the Gothic stone church, whose ruins they are still seen today. Inside these ruins were made excavations by seminarians under the leadership of the lady Cheptea Stela. Excavations have revealed many objects of worship that attest to the existence of Catholics since XV century. The local tradition recorded by the Cotnărean priest Ioan Bărcuță in his report to Holy Congregation of Propaganda Fide in 1676 - claims that this church was built by Margareta Muşata, grandmother of Alexander the Good. In the second half of the nineteenth century the church was covered with dranita and fell prey to the flames of a fire in 1872.

Location

Cotnari, Cotnari village
