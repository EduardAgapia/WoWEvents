Ensemble of Cantacuzino-Paşcanu Mansion

Description

The Ureche family, attested since his time Stephen the Great, sold thoroughbred horses to the Royal Court. At the end of the XVI century, the ruler Moldavia, Petru Şchiopul (1574-1577, 1578-1579 and 1583-1591) gave the boyars Ureche the village of Ceplenița "for 12 good horses, given in the service of the country." Assembly Cantacuzino-Paşcanu mansion was built in year 1600 by Nestor Ureche, father of the chronicler Grigore Ureche. The erection of the mansion happened after he received the Ceplenița estate. Over time, the building had several owners, among whom we list: Grigore Ureche (1628), Anastasia Buhuş, Mihalache Cantacuzino-Paşcanu, Iordache III Cantacuzino (1835), Hristache, George and Eliza Zarifopol (1868). Thus, each boyar family that mastered the precinct brought changes. Initially, the enclosure wall (2m high) and a double-decker mansion with six rooms and cellar were built. In the time of the Buhuș family were added 9 rooms. Around 1835, Iordache III Cantacuzino-Paşcanu built a beautiful palace over the old mansion of the Ear family by adding new rooms and transforming the façade.

Location

Ceplenița
