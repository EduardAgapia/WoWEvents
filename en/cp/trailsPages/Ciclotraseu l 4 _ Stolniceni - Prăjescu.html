Stolniceni-Prajescu Cycloroute

Description

The cycling route is an easier one with a smaller elevation difference due to its positioning geographical towards Lunca Siretului. The starting point is the center of the village (Stolniceni-Prajescu City Hall) and the hydration/refueling place for the route. After, admiring the bust of Matthew Millo and of the tourist information point of the commune, we head to the Orthodox Church of St. Great Martyr George, after which we follow the secondary roads through the fields of cereals to Lunca Siretului. We travel a good part of the route through Lunca Siretului and close to the river bank we can admire the diversity of birds in the meadow. We cross the meadow until we reach the pedestrian bridge from Cozmeşti then turn right towards Sturdza Palace in Cozmeşti and the Church and Cemetery Orthodox also passing by the Zion Church and the Trinity of Miron. From here we head to the village Brătești, where one of the largest and most traditional Lipovan communities in Brăteşti is located county. In the village of Brătești there are many traditional Lipovan houses still well preserved, but also numerous vegetable greenhouses / solariums, the main occupation of the Lipovans. In the center of the village we stop at the wooden Lipovan Church (blue in color), in contrast to the imposing gilded dome of the new church in the immediate vicinity. We hydrate at one of the village shops, then we head on the asphalt road to the wooden Orthodox Church at the entrance to the village of Stolniceni-Prajescu. From here, there are 2 km on the asphalt road to the center of the village (town hall), the end point of this cycloroute. We mention that for people who rarely ride a bike or get tired Quickly, the cycling route can also be shortened to km 11, turning right on the main asphalt road, Cozmeşti – Stolniceni-Prajescu, without going to the village of Brătești.

Route length: 26 km
Vertical climb: 140 m
Maximum altitude: 324 m
Terrain: hilly (with slopes up to 10%)
Road: DJ, DC (partly asphalt, gravel), agricultural and forestry roads
Degree of difficulty: Easy - Medium;
Recommended bike type: eMTB / Mountain-bike / Trekking;
Cyclist profile: Trained amateur/ Families with children/ Children +10 years
Journey time: 3 1/2 - 4 hours;
