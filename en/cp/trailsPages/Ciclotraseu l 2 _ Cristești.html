Cristesti Cycloroute

Description

The cycling route aims to highlight to tourists the main attractions of the commune Cristeşti, starting from the center of the village (Cristești City Hall and Holy Voivodes Orthodox Church). After refueling at the grocery store (hydration point), route Walk through the streets of the village among traditional houses with specific local architecture. We pass by an atypical house inscribed with the insignia of the Dinamo Bucharest football team, then we climb a narrow street that takes us to the village impasse to the relay from where we can admire the village of Cristesti in all its splendor. From here, on farm roads and among cereal fields we head towards the forest of Codrii Pașcanilor. Intersecting the asphalt road we turn left towards Cabana Vânătorească, place for stopping and hydration, after which we head to Homița village, a village located in the heart of Codri Pașcanilor.Arrived In the center of the village, where there is also a traditional fountain, we follow the street of the village Pieruita which leads us to the farm Scufița in Homița, a family of local producers (Antim and Laura) who They grow vegetables by practicing regenerative agriculture. After a small tasting with Little Riding Hood's vegetables from Homita and admiring the traditional houses on this street, we head to the Church Orthodox made of wood, then near the pond in Homița we descend on the asphalt road to the village of Cristesti. Before reaching the center of the village, turn left onto a secondary road to admire the tunnel. railway. Here is also the archaeological site of Cioatele Cristești, a less known place. Of here, on the grassy road, we head towards a traditional sheepfold, up to Cristesti City Hall, the point End of the cycloroute

Trail length: 19 km
Vertical climb: 240 m
Maximum altitude: 417 m
Terrain: hilly (with slopes of over 10%-12%)
Road: DJ, DC (partly asphalt, gravel), forest and logging roads
Degree of difficulty: Medium;
Recommended bike type: eMTB, Mountain-bike/ Trekking;
Cyclist profile: Trained amateur/ Families with children/ Children +12 years
Journey time: 3 1/2 - 4 hours;
