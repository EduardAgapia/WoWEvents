Tătăruşi Curţile Zmeilor Cycloroute

Description

The cycling route from Tătăruși – Curtea Zmeilor aims to highlight the natural and anthropogenic heritage to cyclists from the Tatarus area, also known as Bucovina Mică. The microzone is of special beauty, well-preserved villages with specific architecture (including specific elements of Suceava County), with a technical-municipal infrastructure worthy of envy even and by the administrative-territorial units located in the metropolitan area. The cycling route starts from the area Tatarus Park, located in the heart of the community and in the vicinity of the town hall. After we transit the center Village and admire peasant households, we head to the village of Uda, a village scattered along main road, then turn left on the paved road DJ 133 connecting Tătăruși communes and Cristesti. On the left side runs a beautiful valley and the edge of the Tatarus forest. Way It follows a progressive slope of up to 13% before entering the forest, where the paved road ends and continues with macadam. We pass the intersection with the forest road leading to the Monastery Broşteni (Broşteni County) Suceava), then continue for about another 1 km until the exit from the Tătăruși forest and we see the first households at the end of Homita village. From here, we turn left onto the road that descend about 3 km through the same forest to the Kite Courts Nature Reserve. We admire The beauties of the nature reserve, after which we continue the road to the left that leads us to the village Pietrosu, a village with architecture specific to Bukovina Mici. We turn right at the intersection with DC208F and then left towards the center of Pietrosu village. We stop for a hydration break at one of the village shops, after which we continue our route on the main road that leads us to the center Tătăruși village to Tătăruși Park, the end point of this cycloroute.

Total length: 17 km
Vertical climb: 270 m
Maximum altitude: 420 m
Terrain: hilly (with slopes up to 13%)
Road: DJ and DC (partly asphalt, gravel), logging roads
Degree of difficulty: Medium - Difficult;
Recommended bike type: eMTB / Mountain bike / Trekking;
Cyclist profile: Trained amateur/ Families with children/ Children +12 years
Journey time: 3 - 3 1/2 hours;
