The Church of the Holy Voivodes

Description

The Church of the Holy Voivodes in the village of Cristeşti is a historical monument of national importance (code IS-II-a-A-04112) dating from 1657, having it as founder on Simion Tîrziu. The church is built in Byzantine style, as specific elements we mention: the dome, arches, Byzantine-style arches and semi-arcades. From point of view of the architectural characteristics of construction, it is believed that initially the church would have been a hermitage dependent on the Neamţ Monastery. In the year 1908, the church went through a process of restoration, an inscription being found in Slavonic, translated by the historian Nicolae Iorga: "Faith at Light. This place was made by Simion, the son the poet Ioan Tîrziu in 7165, on April 16". In the nineteenth century, ruler Mihail Sturdza builds at the entrance a bell tower that is kept until 1991. Subsequently, the church is enlarged to be more roomy.

Location

Cristești, Cristești village
